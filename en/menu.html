Amigo serves its steaks natural: straight from Argentina, by the weight you pick, paired with a generous choice of sides. Plus parrillada for two, pasta, pizza, desserts and a Mendoza wine list.

The average guest spends €30 to €45 per person here. That includes a glass of Mendoza Malbec. It doesn't include rushing.

Frequently asked questions

About the menu

Where does Amigo Grill's beef come from?+

Directly from the Argentinian Pampas through our long-standing importer. Amigo serves its steaks natural, with no rubs or marinades, so the flavour of the beef itself takes centre stage.

What exactly is a parrillada?+

An Argentinian tableside grill set-up for two. A small charcoal grill comes to your table. The Parrillada Amigo (€75) includes rump steak, sirloin, spare ribs, chicken breast and chorizo. The Gran Parrillada (€86) adds tenderloin and prawns.

Can I choose the portion size of my steak?+

Yes. All Bifes Argentinos are available in 200g, 300g, 400g, 500g and 1kg (Bife Ancho from 300g, Churrasco only in 500g). Combine with sides of your choice.

Do you have vegetarian options?+

Yes. Jardin de Buenos Aires (€22) is a full Argentinian vegetarian menu. Plus vegetarian pizzas (Mozzarella, Vegetariana, Quattro Formaggi), vegetarian pastas (Spaghetti Agliolio, Penne al Pesto, Lasagna Vegetariana, Canneloni Ricotta) and all the salads.

Do you have a kids menu?+

Yes. Menu para los Niños (€18) is chicken breast, steak or spare ribs with fries and ice cream for dessert.

Which Argentinian wines do you serve?+

A Mendoza-focused list: house wines Michel Torino Malbec, El Molino Chardonnay, Los Pagos Sauvignon and Marival Rosé (glass €6,95 / bottle €31,95). By the bottle from €40: Bodega Navarro Correas Los Árboles, Finca Andinos Merlot, La Mascota Chardonnay and Gran Mascota Malbec, with El Esteco Altimus as the top of the list.

Is the menu also available in English or Spanish?+

Yes. Dish names are kept in Spanish, with English descriptions on this page (and Dutch on the NL site). Our staff speaks Dutch, English and Spanish.
